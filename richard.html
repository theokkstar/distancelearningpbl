Critical Reflection

Richard Zhang

The idea for our advertisement was Distance Learning as the norm. Our target audience was parents and government officials. We selected to advertise Distance Learning as the norm to convey and persuade our target audience that learning from home is more beneficial for families (students and parents) in contrast to the ordinary process of schooling, where we emphasise the negatives which Distance learning can rectify.

Our choice was Distance Learning to be the norm because it was something our whole group could elaborate on without any setbacks and it was something that we experienced which would increase our advertisement’s (e.g. Website’s) credibility ensuring we can use ethos. After pitching our pitch there was critical feedback that influenced changes in our project. Our target audience was refined to government officials (e.g. Department of Education) and parents because they are the people that should be informed and are likely the ones to make the change. We also decided to emphasise on other arguments such as accessibility and wellbeing, rather than highlighting bullying too much.

The Gruen Episodes were an asset that helped inform us on various marketing strategies and developing our creative process. The key factors that were taken away from watching Gruen was how the before and after can be used and how the visual technique aspect of your ad is important (e.g. People, colour etc.). For example, Gruen showed two razor ads with different before and after. One and only showed the after whilst the other add used the before and after side by side. This influenced the creation of the structure of our advertisement. Our group decided to only show the ‘before’ section to promote that there needs to be a change and the scenes we show (such as Will being shoved against a locker) are issues that distance learning can solve.

Other resources that influenced our advertisement were YouTube Tip videos and an exemplar advertisement. For the exemplar ad we analyzed their persuasive strategy, slogan and structure. This ad utilized Grandiose visuals, atmospheric land music and played on the audiences’ expectations by adding a humorous yet dark twist at the end with a slogan “Australia where the bloody hell are you buried?” This allowed us to think about how we would film and edit our advertisement. We used sad/melancholy music and film filters (slightly darker to emphasise sadness) to reach out to the audience's emotion to make them feel there needs to be a movement to distance learning. “The Tips and tricks for making an ad video”. This video had a point that we put more consideration into which was ‘empathize and solve the audiences problem’. This influenced our refinement of the target audience and made us think about what we are trying to achieve in the ad. Our group utilized the ending of the add to ensure the audience can identify “solve the audiences’ problem.” This is shown through statistics and the end card (e.g. Sign the petition, Let's make the transition).
