Critical Reflection

Max Entwistle

For the construction of our advertisement to become media experts and be more critical of our media intake, our group chose to sell the idea of normalising Distance Learning after brainstorming multiple ideas. We then debated our target audience and went through multiple different advertisement plans with target audiences like school kids, the government, teachers, and we finally settled on the idea of empathising with parents.

For our technical strategy, we decided to use a before with no after method after watching an episode of Gruen, having a montage for the before. For the actual content of the advertisement, our group chose to use the idea that parents don't know what's happening to their children at school, as it is relatable to most parents. At the end of our ad, we added an end-card using our slogan and text telling the viewer to sign the petition, a call to action. Throughout the process of planning our ad, multiple resources in class have changed our approach to our ad. The first was the Gruen episodes we watched, as they showed the strategy for a hair loss ad of showing only the after to show how good their product was. From this, we flipped that idea, showing only the before in our ad to show how bad it is. Also from Gruen, we learnt about the use of statistics and the form of credibility it gives. Another ad we saw on Gruen was a comparison of two razors, which made us aware of how good comparisons work. Then we studied some static magazine ads that explored social proxemics and colours to highlight loneliness or contrast features. For example, we looked at a Salvation army ad that showed wealthy people walking past a homeless man in an alleyway. We implemented this idea into the time-lapse of the character Will sitting by himself with hundreds of people walking past him. The next large influence on our strategy was to YouTube video on how to film with a phone. This video gave us some tips to help film more cinematic shots, like the shot of Will snapping his pencil, a fast zoom out into a bird's eye-view that we added to emphasise Will's stress.

Through this whole process of tweaking our strategy and the outside sources that have influenced it, we have learnt a lot about how modern advertisements work. We've been given insight into the behind the scenes of how companies manipulate you into buying their product or idea. Just being aware of the ad's strategy, whether it be ethos, pathos, or logos, is useful for being critical of the media we use today.
